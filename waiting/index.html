About 60,000 people across California are being held in a county jail.

Three quarters of them haven't been convicted or sentenced for a crime.

CalMatters sent record requests to all 58 counties seeking data on long waits in jail. Only 28, which handle about two-thirds of California's felonies, sent it.

In those counties in early 2021, we found about 6,000 unsentenced defendants who have been locked up in jails for more than a year.

At least 1,275 people have been waiting for more than 3 years, including 310 waiting more than 5 years.

Let's turn the spotlight on one or two counties.

There are multiple reasons for the long waits in jail and the slow pace.

The pandemic has delayed trials and forced other court changes, lengthening the waits.

Most detainees waiting for justice are people of color.
